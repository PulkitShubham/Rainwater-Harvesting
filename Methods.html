METHODS OF RAINWATER HARVESTING

Roof catchment method

The roof catchment method of rainwater harvesting is a technique used to collect and store rainwater from rooftops for various purposes, such as irrigation, domestic use, and recharging groundwater. The process involves capturing the rainwater that falls on the roof of a building and channeling it into storage tanks or other storage systems for later use. The roof catchment system typically includes a catchment area, which is the surface of the roof that collects the rainwater, gutters and downspouts, which are used to channel the rainwater from the roof to the storage tank, and a storage tank, which is used to store the collected rainwater.

Surface runoff Collection

Surface runoff collection is a method of rainwater harvesting that involves capturing and storing rainwater as it runs off from roofs, pavements, and other impermeable surfaces. This method is effective in urban areas where there are limited natural surfaces for rainwater to infiltrate into the ground. The basic process involves directing rainwater from rooftops and other surfaces into gutters, downspouts, and pipes that lead to a storage tank or other storage system. The collected water can then be used for non-potable purposes such as irrigation, flushing toilets, and washing clothes.

In Ground Collection

In-ground collection is a method of rainwater harvesting that involves capturing and storing rainwater in underground storage tanks or cisterns. This method is particularly useful in areas with limited space for above-ground storage tanks or where aesthetics are a concern. The basic process involves directing rainwater from rooftops and other surfaces into a series of pipes that lead to an underground storage tank. The collected water can then be pumped out for non-potable uses such as irrigation, washing clothes, or flushing toilets. One advantage of in-ground collection is that it can be used to recharge groundwater by allowing the stored water to slowly infiltrate into the soil over time. This can help to replenish local aquifers and maintain natural water cycles.
